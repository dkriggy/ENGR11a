Remix 3D Files into Novel 3D Prints!

Setptember 12th, 2023

Reflection: My First Attempt at Designing!

Never before had I designed (or modified a preexisting design, in this case) something to be 3D printed! That was what made this second project particularly interesting; I wanted to create something that embraces both style and utility. Thus, I decided to make a play on the Greek mythological story of Atlas who was condemned to hold up the world in his hands for eternity (first image). I decided to add a little weight to his world by including a nifty pencil holder which can easily store a pencil in style. I started out my sketching process with Atlas holding up a globe with a hole drilled through both ends of the globe (second image). My initial thought was the pencil could go right through the globe, in one hole and out the other. I turned this sketch into a design on TinkerCAD by first downloading an Atlas template from www.thangs.com and exporting it to TinkerCAD. I then used the method of subtraction to insert a cylinder through the globe which I then hollowed out of the entire design to create two holes through the globe (third image). I then exported my design from TinkerCAD to PrusaSlicer where I optimized the print settings for this particular print as shown in the fourth and fifth pictures. These print settings definitely achieved my goal. I used 0.2mm quality with Generic PLA and printed on the Prusa MINI+. I was also worried about the structural integrity of the globe because this is what I wanted the pencil to rest on. Thus, I further optimized my print settings to be strong enough to support the pencil’s weight. As such, I decided I needed a 15% infill, which is relatively full, coupled with a minimum shell thickness of 0.5 and 0.7 mm for top and bottom shells, respectively. I used the default nozzle temperature settings which were 215° C for the first layer and 210° C for other layers. As for the settings of the 3D printer itself, the z-offset that I used was -1.253mm. Using these settings optimized towards strength, I printed the design and it came out quite well (sixth picture)! Clearly, it had some stringiness issues (due to the high temperature) which I touched up with a blowtorch (seventh picture), but other than that it printed nearly perfectly. The best part is, it holds the pencil (eighth picture)! The pencil fits nicely through the gap, however it looks a bit awkward and the pencil was not completely secured.

September 12th, 2023

Reflection: My First Ever Design, Second Attempt!

After brainstorming some ideas on how to adapt my prototype, I decided that I needed a more functional and clean looking design. Thus, it was back to the drawing board (literally) and I decided to add a tube this time that ran from hole to hole through the center of the globe (first picture). I took this design once again into TinkerCAD and inserted a cylinder through the globe, superimposed a slightly smaller diameter cylinder through that one and used it to hollow out the larger cylinder, thus creating a tube through the globe (second image). I then exported it to PrusaSlicer again (third image) and printed it out. I did not change my print settings as I liked the previous print settings, and now that I was inserting a tube through the entire design, I did not want to add any unnecessary weight which could potentially crush the globe. The resulting product is shown in the fourth and fifth pictures! This prototype version was mainly focused on the functionality of the pencil holder. It still had the issue of being a bit stringy, so for the future I decided to reduce the nozzle temperature from 210° C to 200° C. Overall, I am very happy with how my second prototype turned out. It combines both functionality and beauty to create quite an eye catching pencil holder!

September 12th, 2023

Reflection: My First Ever Design, Final Product!

The final change to my design was to reduce the stringiness of my Atlas pencil holder! My original nozzle temperature settings were 215° C for my first layer and 210° C for other layers. I needed to reduce the temperature a little bit so that stringiness would stop occurring, but not to the point where the PLA would not stick to the printing bed. As such, I decided to use 210° C for the first layer and 200° C for the other layers (first picture). I did not change anything else about my print settings such as the z-offset, layer height or shell thickness as they were all perfect. I printed it and evidently, my temperature changes in my print settings was a massive success. It came out much, much better! There was very minimal stringiness, and to make my final product perfect I did some post-processing touch-ups with the heat gun (second image). Et voila! The final result is shown (third image)! The final image displays my progress, from right to left. You can notice how the stringiness decreases with each print, as well as the design changes that I implemented along the way! Overall, I am extremely satisfied with my final product and I will be sure to use my snazzy Atlas pencil holder wherever my studies take me!
